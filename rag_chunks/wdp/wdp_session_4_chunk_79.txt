Topic: Revision of State Tools and Business Workflows
Chunk: 79

Explanation:
The lecture focuses on revising the concepts of LAN graph, specifically state tools and business workflows. The teacher explains the purpose and design of states, and provides real-life examples for better understanding. They also discuss what should be stored in a state, what should be temporarily inside a node, and what should be passed to the tool.

Key Points:
1. States are shared runtime context, acting as a passing notepad among all parts.
2. A state can store various types of data such as issue type, urgency, tracking status, final reply, retry count, etc.
3. One-time calculations and some things that do not need to be put inside states should temporarily reside within a node.
4. The teacher will cover examples for better understanding of what should be passed to the tool.

Student Doubts:
1. Clarification on what exactly a state is and what it is not.
2. Question about handling one-time calculations in nodes vs states.
3. Request for more examples to understand what should be passed to the tool.
