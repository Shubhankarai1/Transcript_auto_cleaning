Topic: Handoff Patterns and Topologies

Explanation: The lecture discusses handoff patterns and topologies, focusing on the sequential and cyclic nature of handoffs, topology as a decision-making tool for communication, and various topologies such as pipeline and router topology.

Key Points:
- Handoffs can be sequential or cyclic
- Topology is used to decide communication patterns
- Pipeline topology involves one-way communication
- Router topology involves a central agent for routing queries to relevant agents
- In a router topology, an agent may contain multiple sub-agents with their own topologies

Student Doubts:
- Can there be a return in pipeline topology? (Answer: Yes, sometimes)
- Where should router topology be used? (Answer: When there is only one task at hand, such as an HR request)
- What does the router topology do? (Answer: It routes queries to relevant agents)
- Can multiple sub-agents exist within an agent in a router topology? (Answer: Yes)
- What are states being exchanged in communication? (Answer: The states could be the results of tasks or data related to tasks)
