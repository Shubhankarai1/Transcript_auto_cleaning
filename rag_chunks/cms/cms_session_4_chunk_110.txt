Session: 4
Topic: Customizations Post Retrieval and Transliteration
Chunk: 110

Explanation: In this lecture, the professor discusses customizations that can be made post retrieval in the context of language models (LLM). He also explains transliteration and its use case.

Key Points:
- Customizations after retrieval include post filtering, adding links, shortening context, etc.
- Transliteration is a conversion of English text to Hindi. It can be used when the LLM cannot handle Hindi text directly.
- The idea behind transliteration is to prepare the context for the LLM to ensure it generates appropriate responses in Hindi.

Student Doubts:
1. Can I do translation also in this? (Answer: Yes, you can use transliteration to convert English text into Hindi.)
2. In my program, certain parts are missing during retrieval. Will we do better with advanced chunking and embedding links using lchain and history? (Answer: Yes, the upcoming session will focus on advanced techniques for better results.)
