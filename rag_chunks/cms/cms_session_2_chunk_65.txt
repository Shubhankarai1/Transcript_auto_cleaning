Topic: Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)
Chunk: 65

Explanation: The process of providing context to a language model (LLM) to ensure it provides answers from relevant data sources, also known as grounding.

Key Points:
- Providing context improves the accuracy and relevance of answers
- LLMs need to be provided with augmented information for better performance
- Retrieval of relevant information is necessary before passing it to the LLM
- The process of retrieving and integrating relevant information is called Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)

Student Doubts:
- Why provide context ourselves when the LLM can guess?
- Can't we give entire data sources to the LLM?
- What is the purpose of augmentation in the process?

Answer:
- Providing context helps ensure that answers come from our specific data sources instead of general guesses by the LLM.
- We cannot give entire data sources to the LLM because it would be too much information, so we pull out relevant information for better performance.
- Augmentation refers to the part where relevant information is plugged into the prompt along with historical conversations and the current human message, enhancing the performance of the LLM.
