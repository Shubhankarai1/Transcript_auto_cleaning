Topic: Explanation on Pre-filtering and Post Filtering in Search Algorithms
Chunk: 124

Explanation:
The concept of pre-filtering and post filtering in search algorithms was not initially pioneered by Google. Instead, it is a practice used by several tech giants including Google, IBM, etc., to improve the quality of their search results. Pre-filtering refers to the process of eliminating irrelevant data before further processing, while post filtering involves applying additional criteria after initial processing to refine the results.

Key Points:
- Pre-filtering and post filtering are techniques used by tech companies for better search results.
- Google, IBM, and other premium institutions publish white papers on improving rankings and making answers more effective.
- These techniques are ever-changing as they follow the market trends and white papers released by these institutions.
