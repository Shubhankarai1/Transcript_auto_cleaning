Topic: Explanation of Cross Encoder in Retrieval and Reranking
Chunk: 18

Explanation:
The cross encoder is used to re-rank the documents retrieved from multiple queries using a second opinion model. The process includes generating multiple queries, retrieving multiple documents, applying free ranking, getting fused results, and obtaining reranked results. For debugging purposes, a logger can be added to access document rankings before and after re-ranking.

Key Points:
1. Cross encoder serves as a second opinion model for document re-ranking.
2. The process involves generating queries, retrieving documents, applying free ranking, getting fused results, and obtaining reranked results.
3. A logger can be added for debugging purposes to access document rankings before and after re-ranking.
4. Cross encoder uses the original query, not the multi-query, for the re-ranking process.
5. The cross encoder's importance lies in its ability to improve precision in a recall problem context.
